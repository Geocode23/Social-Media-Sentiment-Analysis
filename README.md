# Sales Performance KPI Analysis using Excel & Power BI

## Project Overview
This project demonstrates the analysis of sales performance data to track key business metrics, identify trends, and provide actionable insights. The project uses **Excel** for data cleaning, pivot tables, and charts, and **Power BI** for creating an interactive KPI dashboard.

The goal is to provide a **comprehensive view of sales performance** across regions, product categories, and customer segments, enabling better decision-making for business growth.

---

## Dataset
The dataset used is a **Superstore Sales dataset** containing the following columns:

- `Order Date` – Date of the order  
- `Region` – Geographic region  
- `Country` – Country of the order  
- `Product Category` – Category of the product  
- `Product Name` – Name of the product  
- `Sales` – Revenue from sales  
- `Profit` – Profit generated  
- `Quantity` – Number of units sold  
- `Customer Segment` – Type of customer (e.g., Corporate, Consumer, Home Office)

> The dataset has been cleaned, with correct data types, no duplicates, and calculated fields such as **Profit Margin** and **Revenue per Order**.

---

## Tools Used
- **Microsoft Excel** – Data cleaning, pivot tables, charts, and basic analytics  
- **Power BI** – Interactive dashboard creation, KPI cards, and visualizations  

---

## Key Features

### Excel Analysis
- Pivot tables for:
  - Total Sales by Region  
  - Total Profit by Product Category  
  - Monthly Sales Trend  
  - Top 10 Products by Sales  
  - Sales by Customer Segment
- Visualizations using:
  - Column charts, line charts, pie charts, and bar charts

### Power BI Dashboard
- KPI Cards:
  - Total Sales  
  - Total Profit  
  - Profit Margin (%)  
  - Total Orders  
  - Average Order Value  
- Visuals:
  - Line chart for Monthly Sales Trend  
  - Bar chart for Profit by Category  
  - Map for Sales by Region  
  - Stacked bar for Sales by Customer Segment  
  - Table for Top 10 Products  
- Interactive slicers for filtering by Date, Region, Category, and Customer Segment

---

## Business Insights
- Certain regions generate high sales but low profit, indicating potential pricing or cost issues.  
- Product categories with the highest profit margins can be prioritized for promotion.  
- Seasonal trends show a sales peak in Q4, which can inform inventory and marketing strategies.  
- Corporate customer segment brings fewer orders but higher revenue per order.  

These insights help businesses make **data-driven decisions** and optimize sales performance.
